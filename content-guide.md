## Writing Guidelines for Future Content

### DO:
- Use person-first language: "persona con VIH" not "VIH positivo"
- Acknowledge emotions without dramatizing: "Es normal sentir miedo"
- Provide clear next steps: "Puedes hacer esto, o esto otro"
- Use "we" when speaking as the platform: "Estamos aqu√≠"
- Be specific about limitations: "No damos consejo m√©dico"

### DON'T:
- Use stigmatizing terms: "infectado", "contagio", "v√≠ctima"
- Make promises you can't keep: "Todo estar√° bien"
- Minimize feelings: "No te preocupes"
- Use medical jargon without explanation
- Create urgency or pressure: "¬°Act√∫a ahora!"

### Accessibility Notes:
- Keep sentences short (max 20-25 words)
- Use common words (8th-grade reading level)
- Break up long text with headings and bullets
- Use active voice
- Explain acronyms on first use (VIH, CCSS, TAR)
